## 1. Executive Summary

**Project Name:** Maya Kingdom Two  
**Type:** Strategy / City-Building / Puzzle Hybrid  
**Platform Target:** PC (Steam), with potential Console ports  
**Target Release:** Q4 2027 (estimated)  
**Team:** NorthernStar Studio

**One-Liner:** "Build, strategize, and survive as you expand your Maya kingdom across new lands — the next evolution in our kingdom-building series."

**Summary:**  
Maya Kingdom Two is a strategic city-building and resource management game where players expand their civilization, manage resources, navigate political intrigue, and grow from a humble village into a thriving metropolis. Building on the success of the original Maya Kingdom, this sequel introduces deeper mechanics, new biomes, enhanced visuals, and a more robust campaign mode. Target audience includes strategy enthusiasts, city-building fans, and players who enjoyed the first installment.

## 2. Market Opportunity

### 2.1 Target Audience

| Segment | Description | Priority |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| Primary | Strategy/City-Building Enthusiasts (18-40) | High |
| Secondary | Fans of original Maya Kingdom (any age) | High |
| Tertiary | History/Mesoamerican culture fans (25-45) | Medium |

### 2.2 Market Fit

- **Current trends:** City-building and strategy games experiencing renewed interest (Palworld, Manor Lords, Frostpunk 2). Indies performing well on Steam. Players seeking depth + accessibility.
- **Gap identified:** Few AAA-quality Maya-themed strategy games. Market underserved for Mesoamerican civilization settings beyond shallow mobile games.
- **Why now:** Proven interest from Maya Kingdom (original). Platform (Steam) matured. Team has domain expertise. Rising interest in diverse historical settings.

### 2.3 Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | Strengths | Weaknesses | Our Differentiation |
|------------|-----------|------------|---------------------|
| **Poly Age** | Strong visuals, broad appeal | Generic setting, shallow mechanics | Maya Kingdom brand + deeper gameplay |
| **Kingdom Two Crowns** | Accessible, pixel charm | Limited scope, mobile-first | More depth, PC-focused |
| **Foundation** | Excellent building freedom | Early access bugs, slow dev | Polished release, narrative campaign |

## 3. Value Proposition

### Core Hook
"Build YOUR Maya kingdom — deeper strategy, richer story, bigger world"

### Key Features (MVP)
1. **Expanded City-Building** — New building types, layouts, and aesthetic options
2. **Resource Management** — Deeper economy with trade, agriculture, and crafting
3. **Campaign Mode** — Story-driven progression with multiple scenarios
4. **New Biomes** — Tropical lowlands, highlands, coastal regions with unique challenges
5. **Enhanced Visuals** — Modern engine, dynamic weather, improved UI/UX
6. **Mod Support** — Map editor and modding tools for community content

### Why Players Will Love It
- **Familiar yet fresh** — Sequel improves on what worked
- **Depth without complexity** — Accessible to new players, rewarding for veterans
- **Unique setting** — No direct Maya city-builder competitors at this quality tier
